#### Cortelazor, a Charming Village in the Sierra de Aracena (Huelva)

**Cortelazor** is a small white village located in the heart of the Sierra de Aracena y Picos de Aroche, in the province of Huelva, Andalusia. With a population of around **300 inhabitants**, it preserves the true essence of traditional rural life in the mountains.

Surrounded by dehesas (oak pastures), chestnut groves, and natural trails, **Cortelazor** is an ideal destination for those seeking peace, nature, and authenticity in the **Sierra de Huelva**. Its residents are officially known as **cortelazoreños**, although they are affectionately nicknamed **“foxes”** (“zorros”).

Its **[heritage](/en/history)**, its **[gastronomy](/en/where-to-eat)**, and its natural surroundings make this village one of the most charming destinations in the **Sierra de Aracena**.

#### History and Heritage of Cortelazor

The old town of **Cortelazor** preserves traces of its past, with narrow streets and traditional mountain architecture. As in many villages of the **Sierra de Huelva**, its heritage is simple yet meaningful.

The **Parish Church of Nuestra Señora de los Remedios** (originally the Church of Saint John the Baptist) stands out as a symbol of the municipality and a meeting point for its inhabitants. Inside, you can find a **Divina Pastora** from the 18th century, a work by the Sevillian painter Alonso Miguel de Tovar.

Old fountains, small squares, its **centenary elm tree**, and history-filled corners are all part of the cultural legacy that can be explored in greater detail in the **[History of Cortelazor](/en/history)** section.
