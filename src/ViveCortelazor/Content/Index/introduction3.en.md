#### Typical Gastronomy of Cortelazor

The **[gastronomy of Cortelazor](/en/where-to-eat)** is deeply connected to the products of the **Sierra de Aracena**.

**Iberian pork** is the foundation of many traditional dishes, along with **acorn-fed Iberian ham**, artisan sausages, grilled meats, and hearty mountain stews.

The village is considered a **nationally recognized mycological enclave** due to the abundance of wild mushrooms, especially *gurumelos*. Added to this are traditional vegetable gardens and homemade preservation techniques, such as bottled tomatoes, intensely seasoned dressings, and artisan liqueurs.

Discover local bars and restaurants in the **[where to eat in Cortelazor](/en/where-to-eat)** section.

#### Culture and Traditions

One of the most important cultural events is the **[National Open-Air Painting Competition of Cortelazor](/en/national-open-air-painting-competition-cortelazor)**, which each year transforms the village into an open-air museum and attracts artists from all over Spain.

Patron saint festivals and other celebrations keep the community spirit alive. You can follow news and events in the **[Cortelazor blog](/en/blog)**.

### Visiting Cortelazor

Visiting **Cortelazor** means discovering one of the most peaceful and authentic villages in the **Sierra de Aracena**, in the province of Huelva.

Whether for a rural getaway, a day of hiking, a gastronomic experience, or simply to enjoy the silence of the mountains, this small Andalusian corner leaves a lasting impression on those who visit.
